## Custom Project

### Dataset

The Kafka producer uses the original sales.csv dataset, which simulates real
time retail transactions. Each record represents a customer purchase with order
details, timestamps, region, product info, pricing, and payment methods.

Fields include:

- order_id
- datetime
- region_id
- currency_code
- product_id
- unit_price
- quantity
- is_online
- customer_id
- is_new_customer
- device_type
- payment_method
- referral_source
- discount_code
- customer_note

Reference tables used for validation and enrichment:

- regions.csv
- products.csv
- currencies.csv
- discount_codes.csv

### Kafka Messages

Each message sent by the producer represents a single sales transaction.

Kafka topic: streaming-06-scenarios-beaderstadt

The message key is region_id, which keeps messages grouped by region in
the same partition.

The base message structure stays the same, but the consumer enriches
each record with calculated fields like subtotal, tax_amount, and total.

### Consumer Processing

The consumer receives sales transaction messages from Kafka and processes
them one at a time.

For each message, the consumer:

- Validates required fields
- Enriches the message with derived fields such as subtotal, tax amount, and total
- Updates running statistics
- Updates a live visualization
- Writes accepted records to both a CSV file and DuckDB database

The consumer logs information about each transaction, including validation
results, calculated totals, and running sales statistics.

Accepted messages are written to consumed_sales_beaderstadt.csv and
stored in DuckDB for additional analysis.

In addition to the standard processing, my consumer tracks customer
revenue totals, regional revenue totals, and revenue generated by new
versus returning customers.

### Experiments

In Phase 4, I extended the consumer by adding real-time aggregation logic.
Instead of only processing individual transactions, the consumer now tracks
top customers, top regions, and revenue generated by new and returning
customers throughout the streaming process. At the end of the run, the
results are exported to a custom JSON analytics summary file.

In Phase 5, I expanded the analytics by adding regional performance metrics.
The consumer now calculates each region's contribution to total revenue,
identifies the top-performing region, and creates a ranked summary showing
the percentage of total revenue generated by each region.

### Results

When I ran everything, all 30 messages were successfully processed and
written into my custom CSV file and DuckDB database.

The analytics summary showed that US-MO was the top-performing region
with $1,252.55 in revenue, accounting for 53.81% of total revenue.
The next highest region was US-TX, contributing 18.60% of total revenue.

The customer analysis also showed that returning customers generated
more revenue than new customers. Returning customers produced
$1,837.51 in sales compared to $490.38 from new customers.

The final JSON summary provided a clear view of top customers,
top regions, customer type revenue, and regional performance rankings.

### Interpretation

Compared to the original example, my project focuses more on analytics
and business insights rather than simply consuming and storing messages.

Throughout this course I've begun to realize how quickly useful metrics can
be generated as data streams in real time with Kafka. Instead of waiting until
the end of a reporting period, businesses can monitor trends and
performance in real time.

The regional performance analysis showed that US-MO was the top-performing region,
generating 53.81% of total revenue. Together, US-MO and US-TX accounted for more
than 72% of all sales, showing that revenue was concentrated in a small number of regions.

The customer analysis also showed that returning customers generated about 79% of
total revenue, making them the primary drivers of sales during the streaming run.

Overall, the consumed messages provided useful business intelligence by identifying
top customers, top-performing regions, and customer purchasing patterns.
These insights could help support decisions related to customer retention
and regional sales strategies.
